# Visa and Entry Basics

## General Guidance

Entry requirements depend on both the traveler's nationality and the
destination country, and they change over time, so this note covers general
concepts rather than country-specific rules. Always confirm current
requirements with the destination country's official immigration authority
or embassy before booking travel, since third-party sites can be outdated.

## Common Entry Categories

Many countries offer visa-free entry or visa-on-arrival for short tourist
stays to citizens of certain other countries, typically for a fixed window
such as 30, 60, or 90 days. Others require an electronic travel
authorization completed online before departure, which is a lighter-weight
process than a traditional visa application but still needs to be done in
advance. Traditional visas, arranged through an embassy or consulate ahead
of time, are more common for longer stays, work travel, or when no
visa-waiver agreement exists between the two countries.

## Passport Validity

A frequently overlooked rule: many countries require a passport to remain
valid for at least six months beyond the planned departure date, not just
through the trip itself. Some also require a minimum number of blank pages
for entry stamps. Checking both of these well before departure avoids
being denied boarding at check-in.

## Supporting Documents

Immigration officers may ask for proof of onward or return travel, proof of
accommodation, and evidence of sufficient funds for the stay, even for
visa-free entry. Travel insurance is mandatory for entry to some
destinations and a good idea everywhere else. It's worth keeping digital
and printed copies of these documents together while traveling.

## Transit Considerations

Even a layover in a country you're not visiting can sometimes require a
transit visa, depending on whether you stay airside, change terminals, or
have a long connection. This is worth checking separately from the entry
requirements of the final destination.
