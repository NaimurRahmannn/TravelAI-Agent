# Packing Tips

## General Principles

Pack around a capsule wardrobe: a small set of interchangeable tops, bottoms,
and one layering piece that can be mixed and matched across the whole trip.
Choose fabrics that resist wrinkling and dry quickly, since laundry access on
the road is often limited. Roll clothing instead of folding it to save space
and reduce creasing, and use packing cubes to keep categories (tops, bottoms,
underwear, electronics) separated and easy to find without unpacking
everything.

## Climate-Specific Packing

For hot and humid destinations, prioritize breathable natural fibers like
cotton and linen, a wide-brim hat, and high-SPF sunscreen, since it can be
harder to find familiar sun-care products abroad. For cold destinations,
layering matters more than a single heavy item: a moisture-wicking base
layer, an insulating mid layer, and a waterproof/windproof outer shell cover
a wider range of conditions than one bulky coat. For destinations with a
rainy season, a compact travel umbrella and a light packable rain shell are
worth the small amount of extra space they take up.

## Documents and Essentials

Keep a photo or scan of your passport, visa, and travel insurance details
stored separately from the physical documents (for example, in cloud
storage), so you have a backup if the originals are lost or stolen. Carry a
small first-aid kit with any personal prescription medication in its
original labeled packaging, plus a basic universal power adapter if
traveling internationally. A portable charger is useful for long travel
days when outlets aren't available.

## Carry-On Strategy

Pack at least one full change of clothes, any essential medication, and
chargers in your carry-on rather than checked luggage, in case checked bags
are delayed or misrouted. If flying with connections, build in slack for
tight layovers and keep boarding passes and identification easily
accessible rather than buried at the bottom of a bag.
